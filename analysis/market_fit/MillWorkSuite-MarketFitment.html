The Revised Core Thesis
The first version of this analysis classified MillworkSuite as a "drawing intake layer" that would hand off to other tools for pricing and proposal. That assessment was incomplete. With a user-configurable pricing module and native proposal generation, MillworkSuite covers the entire pre-production estimating workflow. A millwork estimator using MillworkSuite does not need Bluebeam for takeoff, Alliance Millsoft for pricing, or TradeTek for proposals. MillworkSuite replaces all three — with a purpose-built, AI-accelerated workflow designed exclusively for millwork and casework.

All Six Features — Across All Five Modules

Module 01 · Intake
Auto Page Organisation

Load any architectural PDF and every page is instantly renamed and organised by drawing set. No manual sorting, no renaming, regardless of how many sheets or revisions the set contains.

Module 02 · Scope
AI Elevation Detection

One click scans the entire sheet — room names, drawing scales, and millwork types identified in seconds. Countertops, vanities, base cabinets, upper cabinets — each tagged and ready for review.

Module 02 · Scope
Floor Plan Intelligence

Scans floor plans to detect rooms, walls, and elevation markers. Colour-coded symbols identify identical rooms. Yellow markers link directly to the elevation — every callout connected to its detail sheet automatically.

Module 02 · Scope
Linked Detail & Split View

Click any room to open its elevation alongside the floor plan — dimensions, material callouts, and cross-references all in one synchronized real-time view. Floor plans and elevations linked side by side.

Module 02 · Scope
Calibrated Measure Tool

Verify dimensions and calculate areas directly on the drawing. Distances computed automatically using the page scale — no manual conversion, no scale errors. Confirm every dimension before it enters the estimate.

Module 03 · Pricing
User-Defined Pricing Engine

Users input their own pricing data — material rates, labor rates, overhead — and apply them to the detected scope. Discounts can be customized per line item, per category, or globally. The result is a firm-specific, fully editable price sheet that reflects how each company actually prices millwork.

Module 04 · Proposal
Scope of Work Proposal Generation

The priced estimate generates directly into a professional scope of work proposal ready for GC submission. No re-entry, no copy-paste. The proposal reflects the AI-detected scope, the user's pricing, and can be branded and formatted for client delivery.

Module 05 · Export
Excel & Direct CAD Export

Export to Excel with built-in pricing formulas for further review. Or push directly to Microvellum or CabinetVision — cabinet layouts auto-matched to user-defined libraries, ready to start shop drawings immediately without re-entering a single item.

The Decisive Competitive Picture

vs. Bluebeam Revu
MillworkSuite Replaces Bluebeam for Estimating Teams

Bluebeam is a drawing tool that estimators have adapted into a takeoff tool. It has zero pricing capability and zero proposal output. For millwork estimators, MillworkSuite does what Bluebeam does for scope — automatically and with millwork intelligence — then continues through pricing and proposal without stopping. The only workflow where Bluebeam remains irreplaceable is GC-facing drawing collaboration (Studio Sessions). Recommendation: MillworkSuite is the estimator's platform; Bluebeam Studio is retained for GC coordination only.

vs. Togal.ai
MillworkSuite is More Specific and More Complete

Togal.ai automates floor-plan area takeoff for general construction. It does not classify millwork types, link elevation callouts, connect floor plans to elevation sheets, price detected scope items, or generate proposals. At ~$299/user/month, Togal.ai is an expensive quantity-counting tool for a millwork firm. MillworkSuite covers everything Togal does (within the millwork context) plus pricing, proposals, and CAD export. Togal.ai is a point solution; MillworkSuite is a platform.

vs. Alliance Millsoft
MillworkSuite Adds Everything Upstream Alliance Lacks

Alliance Millsoft is the strongest millwork-native pricing engine on the market. Its dynamic variable pricing (species, finish, hardware, profile) is deeply sophisticated. But it has no drawing import, no AI detection, and no takeoff capability — estimators must manually interpret drawings before they can use it. MillworkSuite delivers the AI-driven upstream workflow that feeds a pricing engine, plus its own user-defined pricing module. For firms not needing Alliance's deepest pricing variables, MillworkSuite replaces it entirely. For firms that want Alliance's pricing depth, MillworkSuite + Alliance Millsoft remains a valid combination.

vs. TradeTek
MillworkSuite Wins on AI; TradeTek Wins on Pricing Depth (Currently)

TradeTek's Millworks Bundle is the most complete millwork-native estimating tool after Alliance Millsoft — it handles takeoff assemblies, cost databases, and bid proposals. Its weakness is manual drawing import and no AI detection. MillworkSuite's AI scope discovery and CAD export are decisive advantages. The battleground is the pricing module: TradeTek has pre-built millwork cost assemblies; MillworkSuite has user-defined pricing. As MillworkSuite's pricing library matures, TradeTek's primary advantage diminishes.

vs. INNERGY
MillworkSuite is the Best-of-Breed Estimating Alternative to Full ERP

INNERGY is the right choice for firms ready to commit to a full ERP transformation — estimating through shop floor, job costing, and manufacturing. The implementation is heavy, the cost is high, and the process change is significant. MillworkSuite is the right choice for firms that want best-in-class estimating and proposal generation without rebuilding their entire business around a new ERP. These products serve different stages of business maturity, not competing head-to-head for the same buyer.

vs. Cabinet Vision / Microvellum
MillworkSuite Feeds Them — Not a Competitor

Cabinet Vision and Microvellum are design-to-manufacture tools. They are the shop's production system. MillworkSuite's direct CAD export with library pre-matching makes it the natural estimating front-end for shops that already run Cabinet Vision or Microvellum. The workflow becomes: MillworkSuite (estimate + proposal) → Cabinet Vision or Microvellum (design + CNC). No firm needs to choose between them.
