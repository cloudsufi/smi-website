Auto Page Organisation

Any architectural PDF is instantly renamed and organized by drawing set — no manual sorting, no matter how many sheets or revisions.

Feature 02
AI Elevation Detection

Verify dimensions and calculate areas directly on drawing. Distances computed automatically using page scale — no manual conversion, no scale errors.

Excel with built-in pricing formulas for cost review. Or direct push to Microvellum or CabinetVision — cabinet layouts auto-matched to user-defined libraries, ready for shop drawings immediately.

Where MillworkSuite Fits in the Market
Core Finding
MillworkSuite occupies a category that does not currently exist in the millwork software market: an AI-native, millwork-specific drawing intake and scope discovery layer with direct CAD handoff. It is not a general takeoff tool, not a pricing engine, and not a design/production system. It is the bridge between drawing receipt and everything downstream — and that bridge has never been built specifically for millwork.

Suggested Pricing Strategy for MillworkSuite

Anchor Point

Price Between Bluebeam and Togal.ai

Bluebeam Core ($330/yr = $27/mo) is the "cheap but generic" comparison. Togal.ai (~$299/mo) is the "expensive AI generalist" comparison. MillworkSuite should price at $150–$250/user/month — premium over Bluebeam, but justified by millwork specificity; below Togal.ai, justified by CAD handoff value that Togal can't deliver.

Value Metric

Charge by Seat + Volume of Drawing Sets

A per-user seat license plus a usage tier (projects processed per month) captures value efficiently. Small shops processing 5–10 bids/month pay less than large commercial firms processing 30–50 drawing packages monthly. Consider: Starter ($149/user/mo, up to 15 sets/mo), Professional ($249/user/mo, up to 50 sets/mo), Enterprise (custom).

ROI Proof

Time-to-Value Must Be Under 2 Hours

If MillworkSuite saves 4+ hours per drawing package and the estimator's fully-loaded cost is $60–$100/hour, the tool pays for itself on the first project. ROI framing: "One use saves $240–$400 in estimator time." Monthly sub at $200 pays back in less than one job.

Bundle Path

Partner with Alliance Millsoft / INNERGY for Bundled Pricing

Offering bundled pricing with downstream estimating tools (Alliance Millsoft, INNERGY) creates a full workflow solution, reduces churn, and accelerates adoption through partner channels. A bundle at $350–$450/user/month for MillworkSuite + Alliance Millsoft could displace Bluebeam + spreadsheet entirely.

Go-to-Market Implications
Primary ICP

Mid-Size U.S. Architectural Millwork Firms

  • 5–25 employees, $5M–$30M revenue
  • 1–3 estimators receiving 20–60 drawing packages/month
  • Currently using Bluebeam + Excel + Microvellum or Cabinet Vision
  • Pain: hours lost to PDF sorting, elevation hunting, manual CAD re-entry
  • Ready to pay: $150–$300/user/month if ROI is clear
Secondary ICP

Commercial Casework Shops with CNC

  • Own a Cabinet Vision or Microvellum license
  • Struggle to get from drawing to shop drawing without manual re-entry
  • MillworkSuite's CAD export is the direct value prop
  • Can be sold as "the missing front-end" to CV and Microvellum
Positioning Statement

"The First Tool You Open, Not the Last"

MillworkSuite is positioned as the drawing intake layer — what happens in the first 2 hours after drawings arrive. Everything downstream becomes faster because MillworkSuite structured the scope first. This avoids competing with Bluebeam on collaboration and with Alliance Millsoft on pricing.

Channel Strategy

AWI Members, Microvellum & Cabinet Vision Resellers

  • AWI (Architectural Woodwork Institute): 400+ member firms are the exact ICP
  • Microvellum resellers: bundle MillworkSuite as the "drawing front-end" for new installs
  • Cabinet Vision resellers (Hexagon partner network)
  • INNERGY partnership for end-to-end stack marketing
Summary Verdict
MillworkSuite is entering a category that doesn't exist, with features no competitor has, targeting a market that is definitively underserved. Its risk is not product differentiation — the differentiation is clear. Its risk is market education (customers don't know they're missing a drawing intake layer) and the downstream gap (scope without pricing still requires a second tool). The go-to-market must focus relentlessly on time savings at drawing intake, not on competing with estimating or design tools.
